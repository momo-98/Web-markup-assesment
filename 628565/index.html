Hoofddorp

What is Hoofddorp?

Hoofddorp is the largest village in Haarlemmermeer. It has 73,496 inhabitants and is very good and easy to reach by car and public transport. In Hoofddorp there is (Het Cultuurgebouw). there is a large and partly covered shopping center (Hoofddorp Winkelstad) and there are various business and office areas.

Hoofddorp history

After the reclamation of the Haarlemmermeer it was decided in 1853 to create two villages. These two villages ( Kruisdorp and Venneperdorp ) were situated in the middle of the available area. On July 11 in 1855 the Haarlemmermeer became an independent city . At the municipal meeting of November 12 in 1868 it was decided to be called Kruisdorp (Hoofddorp) then. A cross village already existed in Zeeland. In the same year, Venneperdorp was renamed Nieuw-Vennep. The development of Hoofddorp went more quickly than that of Nieuw-Vennep. It soon became the most important village in the municipality, although Badhoevedorp (also located in the Haarlemmermeer) had more inhabitants for a long time.

Toolenburg

What is Toolenburg

Toolenburg is a residential area in the province (North Holland). It contains two parts (Toolenburg West) and (Toolenburg East) and the government is working on the third part (Toolenburg South). Toolenburg is also famous for it's sport clubs and it's Toolenburgerplas. Toolenburg also has several sports clubs (ZPCH and SV Hoofddorp). There are tennis fields, a baseball field, a fitness Gym and an indoor intertainment center, called (het Spectrum). Unfortunately, this is no longer available at the moment. This center includes a restaurant, an indoor swimming pool with sunbathing lawn, fitness center and sports hall, for gymnastics, basketball or badminton, among other things.

Toolenburgerplas

The Toolenburgerplas is the paradise for sun lovers and "non-motorized water sports enthusiasts". Here you can swim, surf and sunbathe. And of course also cycling and walking. The Toolenburgerplas can be reached from sunrise to sunset.The Toolenburgerplas was officially opened on 31 May 2010 and is managed by the municipality of Haarlemmermeer. The domain consists of a park area, a nature reserve and an area for shore and water recreation that is popular with surfers and divers. The hiking trail around the lake is 3.2 km long. In 2013, the area attracted around 1.4 million visitors in total. At Toolenburg there has been a recreational area of 85 hectares since 2003, with a small lake: the Toolenburgerplas. The hiking trail around the lake is 3.2 km long.

Haarlemmermeerse Bos

overall In Haarlemmermeer you will find many recreational areas that are very suitable for a day out in the outdoors. Such as the Haarlemmermeerse Bos. The Haarlemmermeerse Bos is 115 hectares in size. It is a park-like area around a recreational lake to the north-west of Hoofddorp. There are several restaurants where you can relax after a nice walk or a refreshing swim. The forest is extra beautiful because of the many remains of the Floriade 2002.
